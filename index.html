Research

Minimum Wage Effects on Employment and Earnings: Evidence from Linked Employer-Employee Data Job Market Paper
Draft available upon request  ·  2024
This paper uses a difference-in-differences design exploiting variation in state-level minimum wage changes to estimate effects on low-wage employment and earnings. Using linked employer-employee data from [country], I find that moderate minimum wage increases raise earnings with limited disemployment effects, particularly in tradeable sectors.

Unemployment Insurance Generosity and Job Search Behavior Working Paper
With [Coauthor Name]  ·  2023
We exploit a regression-discontinuity design around benefit eligibility thresholds to estimate the effect of UI benefit duration on job search intensity and reemployment outcomes. Our estimates suggest modest moral hazard effects, which are outweighed by consumption smoothing benefits.
Paper (PDF) SSRN
Occupational Licensing and Wage Inequality Across Gender Lines
Work in progress  ·  2024
This project investigates whether the expansion of occupational licensing requirements has differentially affected male and female workers in licensed professions, contributing to observed gender wage gaps within occupations.
